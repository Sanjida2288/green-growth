ABOUT GREEN INNOVATORS

Bangladesh is vulnerable to disasters induced by climate change, such as floods and high tidal waves leading to loss of livelihood and suffering from environmental pollutions, while vulnerable groups are disproportionately affected by the disasters and environmental degradation. The Global Climate Risk Index ranks Bangladesh as the world’s seventh most affected country over the period 1999-2018. Rising temperatures leading to more intense and unpredictable rainfalls during the monsoon season and a higher probability of catastrophic cyclones are expected to result in increased tidal inundation. It is estimated that a one-meter rise in sea levels would submerge 18 percent of arable land in coastal areas. Recent studies estimate that by 2050 Bangladesh could have 13.3 million internal climate migrants. Additional rural-urban migration would have significant consequences for air and water pollution and unsustainable consumption of natural resources while putting additional pressure on urban labor markets. Addressing climate risks is increasingly urgent to ensure the sustainable economic development of the country.

Tackling Climate Change induced damage and loss (Climate change adaptation)

The adverse impacts and irreversible harm caused by climate change cannot be mitigated or adapted. These include the destruction of ecosystems, loss of biodiversity, rising sea levels, extreme weather events, displacement of communities, and economic losses. Addressing climate change loss and damage is a complex and challenging task that requires a multi-faceted approach involving international cooperation, policy changes, financial mechanisms, and community resilience building.

WHY NECESSARY

Bangladesh is committed to act for addressing climate change. Using the principles of Green growth, jobs for Green Technology and Green Industries will present considerable opportunities for job creation and skills enhancement. The transition to a green economy in Bangladesh leads to substantial changes in policies, regulations, technologies, business practices, green and climate financing opportunities among others.

The increasingly environmentally conscious global supply chains have also been impacting the adoption of green technology, green finance models and innovation in Bangladeshi industries, leading to greater demand for green tech, knowledge, skills and jobs. People are also becoming more aware of climate vulnerability and the need for greening measures, which is affecting consumer behavior at large. These factors give Bangladesh industries a strong incentive in becoming the supplier for ecologically safe products and services keeping ecological footprint minimum through the production cycle. The recently completed Skills and Education for Greener Bangladesh identifies a range of green jobs created in key industries of Bangladesh.
