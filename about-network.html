Our Mission & Vission

The objectives of green technology online platform are:

a.To develop a strong base on skills and education for green growth, green jobs, environment focused IR4 and futuristic technological development in Bangladesh through sharing of knowledge, information, expertise and best practices with education and skills sectors;

b.To establish a global online platform on green technologies, research and innovations, education and skills for Bangladeshi (including diaspora) professionals, experts, educationist, scientist, practitioners and learners living home and abroad for sharing knowledge, best practice, transition and new green job opportunities;

c. To build institutional capacity for promoting green growth and green job generation through training content development, capacity building of the teachers, green experts, policymakers, and future climate leaders; and

d.To create awareness on green technologies, new green job opportunities, jobs in transition, job redundancies, effect of climate change and global warming through social media.

ABOUT NETWORK

Bangladesh is vulnerable to disasters induced by climate change, such as floods and high tidal waves leading to loss of livelihood and suffering from environmental pollutions, while vulnerable groups are disproportionately affected by the disasters and environmental degradation. The Global Climate Risk Index ranks Bangladesh as the world’s seventh most affected country over the period 1999-2018. Rising temperatures leading to more intense and unpredictable rainfalls during the monsoon season and a higher probability of catastrophic cyclones are expected to result in increased tidal inundation. It is estimated that a one-meter rise in sea levels would submerge 18 percent of arable land in coastal areas. Recent studies estimate that by 2050 Bangladesh could have 13.3 million internal climate migrants. Additional rural-urban migration would have significant consequences for air and water pollution and unsustainable consumption of natural resources while putting additional pressure on urban labor markets. Addressing climate risks is increasingly urgent to ensure the sustainable economic development of the country.

Bangladesh is committed to act for addressing climate change. Using the principles of Green growth, jobs for Green Technology and Green Industries will present considerable opportunities for job creation and skills enhancement. The transition to a green economy in Bangladesh leads to substantial changes in policies, regulations, technologies, business practices, green and climate financing opportunities among others.

The increasingly environmentally conscious global supply chains have also been impacting the adoption of green technology, green finance models and innovation in Bangladeshi industries, leading to greater demand for green tech, knowledge, skills and jobs. People are also becoming more aware of climate vulnerability and the need for greening measures, which is affecting consumer behavior at large. These factors give Bangladesh industries a strong incentive in becoming the supplier for ecologically safe products and services keeping ecological footprint minimum through the production cycle. The recently completed Skills and Education for Greener Bangladesh identifies a range of green jobs created in key industries of Bangladesh.

However, the discourse around green growth and green jobs in the country is severely suffering from the lack of available evidence, which leads to inadequate policy actions, institutional cross ministry dialogue and investment in enabling capacity taken by the government. This gap is made worse by limited awareness, and technical knowledge and understanding of the needs of private sector in order to promote green industry leading to jobs generation and green skills development. Currently, the visibility of green jobs and skills for green jobs are low and lack of identification of green jobs or practices. The institutional roles and capacity and awareness on green jobs generation and developing skills & knowledge for green jobs is still deficient in Bangladesh. It needs substantial strengthening for the country to ensure the future green growth of the Bangladesh economy. There have been efforts to enhance the supply of green skills and knowledge and boost policy support for green technology investment and green jobs generation. However, those are still at nascent stages, facing various challenges, and require innovative approaches to leapfrog them into the next steps

The World Bank aims to create much-needed evidence and demonstration for policy, investment and capacity needs to support green skills and jobs. Components will include analysis, data collection, policy dialogue between government bodies and stakeholders (private sector). This will also include knowledge sharing with countries that have implemented green growth policies (South Korea, Columbia, Vietnam) and demonstration of green technology, green business models, and how education and skills need to be supported by institutional roles (both existing and to be identified), and capacity building on the current situation of green jobs and skills provision in Bangladesh. There are hundreds of expatriate Bangladeshi scholars on Green Technologies and Green Growth professionals working in many universities and institutions across the world. The World Bank as a part of a grant wants to establish an online platform and organize seminars, and workshops, and generating policy recommendations for integrated green growth of Bangladesh. These activities are expected to benefit environmental professionals across the countries.
